The **1987 Major League Baseball season** ended with the American League Champion Minnesota Twins winning the World Series over the National League Champion St. Louis Cardinals, four games to three, as all seven games were won by the home team.

In June, future Hall of Fame outfielder Ken Griffey Jr. was selected with the number one overall pick in the Major League Baseball draft, by the Seattle Mariners.

## Events

- January 14 тАУ Catfish Hunter and Billy Williams are elected to the Hall of Fame by the Baseball Writers' Association of America.
- March 3 тАУ Ray Dandridge, a third baseman from the Negro leagues, is the only player elected to the Hall of Fame by the Special Veterans Committee.
- April 13 тАУ At Jack Murphy Stadium, the San Diego Padres set a major league record when the first three batters in the bottom of the first inning hit home runs off San Francisco Giants starter Roger Mason in their home opener. The Padres, trailing 3тАУ0, got homers from Marvell Wynne, Tony Gwynn and John Kruk. Despite this, the Padres lost 13-6. This record would be matched in 2003.
- April 15 тАУ Juan Nieves of the Milwaukee Brewers pitches a no-hitter against the Baltimore Orioles. He becomes the second-youngest pitcher in major league history to accomplish the feat, and the first Brewer to do so.
- April 17 тАУ Mike Schmidt of the Philadelphia Phillies hits the 500th home run of his career. It came in the ninth inning against the Pittsburgh Pirates' Don Robinson, giving the Phillies an 8тАУ6 win.
- June 2 тАУ The Seattle Mariners use the number-one overall pick of the draft to select Ken Griffey Jr., signaling a turnaround in their fortunes as an organization.
- June 28 тАУ Don Baylor of the visiting Boston Red Sox is hit by a pitch from Rick Rhoden in the sixth inning of a 6тАУ2 win over the New York Yankees. The HBP gives Baylor 244 for his career, breaking Ron Hunt's modern-day record.
- July 14 тАУ Tim Raines caps a 3-for-3 performance in the All-Star Game with a two-run triple in the top of the 13th inning, giving the National League a 2тАУ0 victory over the American League. Raines is selected the MVP.
- July 8тАУ18 тАУ New York Yankees first baseman Don Mattingly tied a Major League record and broke an American League record by hitting home runs in eight consecutive games, in a 7тАУ2 loss to the Texas Rangers. He tied the record set by Dale Long in 1956.
- August 11 тАУ Mark McGwire of the Oakland Athletics breaks Al Rosen's American League rookie record by hitting his 38th home run in an 8тАУ2 loss to the Mariners.
- August 26 тАУ Paul Molitor of the Milwaukee Brewers goes hitless, and ends his 39-game hitting streak; it is the longest American League hitting streak since Joe DiMaggio's 56-game streak in 1941.
- August 30 тАУ With knuckleball pitcher Charlie Hough on the mound, Texas Rangers catcher Geno Petralli ties a Major League record by committing six passed balls in a 7тАУ0 loss to the Detroit Tigers at Tiger Stadium. All seven runs are unearned and come as a result of the passed balls. Petralli will go on to commit 35 passed balls on the season, breaking J. C. Martin's single-season record of 33 in 1965.
- September 9 тАУ Nolan Ryan strikes out 16 to pass 4,500 for his career as the Houston Astros beat the San Francisco Giants 4тАУ2. Ryan strikes out 12 of the final 13 batters and fans Mike Aldrete to complete the seventh inning for his 4,500th strikeout.
- September 14 тАУ In the midst of the Toronto Blue Jays' 18тАУ3 drubbing of the Baltimore Orioles, Cal Ripken Jr. is lifted from the lineup and replaced by Ron Washington, stopping Ripken's consecutive innings played streak at 8,243.
- September 18 тАУ Darrell Evans hits his 30th home run of the season, and becomes the first player to do so after the age of 40.
- September 21 тАУ Darryl Strawberry swipes his 30th base of the season to join the 30тАУ30 club. With teammate Howard Johnson already having joined, it marks the first time that two teammates achieve 30тАУ30 seasons in the same year.
- September 22 тАУ Wade Boggs of the Boston Red Sox reaches the 200-hit mark for the fifth straight season in an 8тАУ5 loss to the Detroit Tigers.
- October 4 тАУ The Detroit Tigers defeat the Toronto Blue Jays 1тАУ0 to clinch the American League East division title. The victory caps off a thrilling pennant race in which the Tigers overcame a 3.5 game deficit to the Blue Jays in the last two weeks of the season, including sweeping the Blue Jays at Tiger Stadium in the final weekend, and finishing two games ahead of Toronto in the standings.
- October 25 тАУ In Game Seven of the World Series, starter Frank Viola and reliever Jeff Reardon hold the St. Louis Cardinals to six hits, as the Minnesota Twins win 4тАУ2 for their second World Championship, and their first since moving to Minnesota and changing their name to the Twins. The franchise's first title came in 1924 as the Washington Senators. Viola is named the Series MVP.
- November 18 тАУ Andre Dawson of the Chicago Cubs is announced as the winner of the National League MVP Award, becoming the first recipient of the award to play for a last place team.
