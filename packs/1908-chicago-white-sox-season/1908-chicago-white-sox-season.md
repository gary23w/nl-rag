# 1908 Chicago White Sox season

The 1908 season was the eighth in **Chicago White Sox** history and its eighth as a major league team. Owner Charles Comiskey optioned land in the summer of 1908 for what would become Comiskey Park. Despite ace pitcher Ed Walsh going an incredible 40тАУ15 in 1908, the Sox could only muster a third-place finish in the American League standings, behind Detroit and Cleveland, ultimately finishing 88тАУ64. The White Sox hit only three home runs for the entire season, the lowest total for a major league team in the modern era (since 1901).
