## Events

### February

- February 11 тАУ The Chicago Cubs trade Johnny Evers, part of the legendary Tinkers to Evers to Chance infield, to the Boston Braves for Bill Sweeney.
- February 19 тАУ Solly Hofman jumped from the Pittsburgh Pirates to Brooklyn Tip-Tops of the Federal League.
- February 27 тАУ Jack Quinn, a pitcher for the Boston Braves, jumped from the National League to the Baltimore Terrapins of the Federal League.

### April

- April 17 тАУ Pitcher Red Faber makes his MLB debut for the Chicago White Sox in their 6тАУ5 victory over the winless Cleveland Naps.
- April 21 тАУ Future Hall of Famer Frank Chance plays his final game, entering as a defensive replacement for the New York Yankees in the bottom of the 9th inning.
- April 30 тАУ Nick Cullop jumps from the Cleveland Naps to the Kansas City Packers.

### May

- May 31 тАУ Joe Benz pitches a no hitter in a 6тАУ1 Chicago White Sox victory over the Cleveland Naps.

### June

- June 3 тАУ The Baltimore Terrapins trade Happy Finneran to the Brooklyn Tip-Tops in exchange for Felix Chouinard.
- June 9 тАУ Honus Wagner of the Pittsburgh Pirates becomes the second member of the 3000 hit club.

### July

- July 1 тАУ Harry Kingman pinch-hits for the New York Yankees in their 7тАУ4 loss to the Washington Senators, becoming the first Chinese-born major league baseball player.
- July 9 тАУ The Boston Red Sox purchase the contracts of Ernie Shore, Ben Egan and Babe Ruth from the Baltimore Orioles of the International League for $25,000.

### August

- August 27 тАУ Fred McMullin makes his major league debut for the Detroit Tigers in their 9тАУ2 loss to the Boston Red Sox.
- August 28 - One day after being released by the Boston Red Sox, infielder Steve Yerkes jumps to the Federal League, signing with the Pittsburgh Rebels.

### September

- September 9 тАУ In the second game of a doubleheader, George Davis of the Boston Braves pitches a no-hitter against the Philadelphia Phillies in a 7тАУ0 win.
- September 19 тАУ Ed Lafitte pitches a no-hitter for the Brooklyn Tip-Tops of the Federal League in a 6тАУ2 win over the Kansas City Packers.
- September 21 тАУ Walter Johnson of the Washington Senators has four wild pitches in an inning.
- September 27 тАУ Nap Lajoie of the Cleveland Naps becomes the third member of the 3,000 hit club.

### October

- October 13 тАУ The Boston Braves defeat the Philadelphia Athletics 3тАУ1 in Game 4 of the 1914 World Series to win their first World Series, four games to none, for the first four-game sweep in World Series history.
- October 18 тАУ Pitcher Hugh Bedient of the Boston Red Sox jumps to the Buffalo Blues of the Federal League.

### November

- November 16 тАУ Eddie Plank reportedly jumps from the Philadelphia Athletics of the American League to the Federal League's Chicago Federals after Athletics owner Connie Mack instigates a fire sale, asking waivers for Plank, Jack Coombs, and Chief Bender.

### December

- December 2 тАУ Pitcher Eddie Plank officially jumps to the Federal League, signing a contract with the St. Louis Terriers.
- December 5 тАУ Pitcher Chief Bender jumps to the Federal League, signing a contract with the Baltimore Terrapins.
