# Acura ARX-05

The **Acura ARX-05** is a Daytona Prototype International (DPi) sports prototype racing car developed by Honda Performance Development (HPD) in collaboration with Oreca. The car began use in 2018 and was designed in accordance with IMSA’s DPi regulations which had debuted the year prior, being based on the Oreca 07 LMP2 chassis. The ARX-05 first competed in the IMSA Weathertech Sportscar Championship in a factory-backed effort with Team Penske before being raced by customer teams Wayne Taylor Racing and Meyer Shank Racing.

The ARX-05 marked Acura’s return to competition in North American prototype racing following previous programs under the HPD name. In its five seasons of competition from 2018 to 2022, the ARX-05 would find consistent success in IMSA competition, being a regular fixture in championship fights and winning a number of races.

With DPi regulations replaced by Le Mans Daytona h (LMDh) regulations from 2023 onwards, the ARX-05 was retired and replaced by the Acura ARX-06.

## Development

### Background

HPD's previous efforts in IMSA prototype competition had ended under difficult circumstances. In 2014, the ARX-03, a predecessor to the ARX-05, was withdrawn from later races by Extreme Speed Motorsports due to complaints regarding IMSA’s Balance of Performance (BoP). By 2015, with Extreme Speed Motorsports reducing its IMSA campaign to endurance events only, HPD's participation in the series was likewise limited with the newly developed ARX-04b.

The ARX-04b proved to be a significant failure, competing only in the 2015 24 Hours of Daytona before being shelved for the older ARX-03, which itself would contest only one additional IMSA race before Extreme Speed Motorsports cancelled the remainder of its 2015 campaign. The ARX-04b would not compete again despite continued development and testing.

Hoping to move on from previous setbacks, HPD would begin preparations for its next IMSA prototype as new regulations for LMP2 prototypes for 2017 were finalized alongside IMSA’s DPi ruleset. With the car being run under the Acura brand for the first time since 2011, HPD conducted multiple interviews with multiple different chassis manufacturers before ultimately reaching an agreement with Oreca to utilize the Oreca 07 as the basis for the ARX-05. HPD and Acura’s design team would work in tandem to develop the car.

Acura’s return to IMSA prototype competition was formally announced on 11 July 2017, with HPD having reached an agreement with Team Penske to campaign the car from the 2018 season onwards. The ARX-05 was publicly unveiled at Laguna Seca on 18 August 2017.

### Specifications and design

The ARX-05 is constructed around the Oreca 07’s carbon-fiber monocoque chassis. Acura-specific bodywork and exterior styling were developed by Acura Design Studio in California in collaboration with HPD and Oreca engineers. The car incorporates Acura’s signature “Jewel Eye” headlight styling from their production cars while retaining the standardized cockpit dimensions and survival cell structure mandated by the new LMP2 regulations for all DPi entries.

Power is supplied by the Acura AR35TT, also known as the Honda HR35TT, a twin-turbocharged 3.5-liter V6 engine developed and assembled by HPD. The engine had previously been used in endurance prototypes competing under differing regulations, including the Ligier JS P2 and Riley MkXXVI. The AR35TT is derived from Honda's J35 production engine found in multiple road cars. Power output is regulated by IMSA BoP adjustments in a rear mid-engine, rear wheel drive layout.

The ARX-05 features a paddle-shifting six-speed sequential gearbox supplied by Xtrac alongside an independent double-wishbone pushrod suspension at all four corners. Braking is provided by Brembo six-piston brake calipers acting on carbon brake rotors.

### Testing

The initial shakedown of the ARX-05 was carried out at Circuit Paul Ricard in late July 2017, prior to the car’s official public unveiling. Footage of the test was later used in a teaser released by Acura on August 8. Testing efforts expanded following the car’s transport to the United States.

The ARX-05 completed its first North American test at Road Atlanta on 25 August 2017, with Juan Pablo Montoya driving over the course of two days. The car later completed its first test on Daytona International Speedway's banking on 3 November 2017, a month ahead of IMSA's official test days at the track in December.

## Racing history

### Team Penske (2018-2020)

#### 2018

For the ARX-05's debut season in the 2018 IMSA SportsCar Championship, Team Penske would campaign two entries: the No. 6 ran by Juan Pablo Montoya and Dane Cameron full-time with Simon Pagenaud in for endurance rounds and the No. 7 ran by Helio Castroneves and Ricky Taylor full-time with Graham Rahal in for endurance rounds.

Both cars would achieve moderate success throughout the season. Mechanical problems and race damage would plague both teams at the 24 Hours of Daytona and 12 Hours of Sebring, preventing consistent results among the Prototype class. Montoya would secure the team's first pole position at Long Beach as performance steadily improved. The breakthrough came at Mid-Ohio, where the ARX-05 achieved its maiden victory with the No. 7 of Castroneves and Taylor leading a 1-2 finish over the No. 6. Following another strong showing at Belle Isle, a double podium, Team Penske would struggle to maintain the same level of success for the rest of the season. The No. 6 would record additional podium finishes but continued to suffer from reliability problems. The No. 7 would fail to return to the podium after Belle Isle, stringing together multiple poor results. The team led the most laps at the Petit Le Mans, but a late race drive-through penalty dropped them to 5th.

The No. 6 and No. 7 teams would finish 5th and 7th in team standings respectively, with Acura finishing 2nd in Manufacturer standings in their debut season with the ARX-05.

#### 2019

For the 2019 IMSA SportsCar Championship, Team Penske retained the No. 6 lineup of Juan Pablo Montoya, Dane Cameron, and Simon Pagenaud for the endurance rounds. Alexander Rossi replaced Graham Rahal as the No. 7's third driver for the endurance races, with Rahal returning only for Petit Le Mans.

The ARX-05 would find greater success in 2019 following its inconsistent debut season. The No. 6 encountered early setbacks at the 24 Hours of Daytona and 12 Hours of Sebring, finishing sixth and ninth respectively. The No. 7, meanwhile, would achieve a podium at Daytona. A double podium at Long Beach would mark a turnaround for Team Penske, with the No. 6 rebounding to record seven straight podium finishes, including three wins at Mid-Ohio, Belle Isle, and Laguna Seca respectively. The No. 7 failed to record a victory but stayed in championship contention, achieving five podiums with only one finish below 5th. The ARX-05 saw three more double podiums throughout the year.

The No. 6 became IMSA's 2019 champion for team competition while the No. 7 finished in 3rd. Acura, meanwhile, won their first manufacturer's championship in DPi and the first with the ARX-05.

#### 2020

The driver lineups remained unchanged for the 2020 IMSA SportsCar Championship, with the only change being Alexander Rossi serving as the No. 7's sole driver for endurance rounds.

The fortunes of the two Team Penske entries varied greatly throughout 2020. The No. 6 and No. 7 finished 4th and 8th respectively at the 24 Hours of Daytona prior to the postponement of the season due to the COVID-19 pandemic. Following IMSA's return to racing at the Weathertech 240, the No. 6 would string together multiple mediocre finishes, rebounding later on in the season to achieve three podiums in the final three races. The No. 7, following poor results at the Weathertech 240 and Sebring, dominated the latter half of the season, winning four out of the last six races.

Acura and Team Penske successfully defended their titles in 2020, with the No. 7 winning the team's championship by one point and Acura narrowly edging out Cadillac to take the manufacturer's championship. The No. 6 would only finish 7th in the team's championship due to inconsistency.

The 2020 season also marked the final campaign of the Acura Team Penske partnership. It was announced on 20 July 2020 that they would part ways following the conclusion of the season after three years of competition.

### Wayne Taylor Racing and Meyer Shank Racing (2021-2022)

#### 2021

Beginning in the 2021 IMSA SportsCar Championship, factory support for the ARX-05 was assumed by Wayne Taylor Racing and Meyer Shank Racing, the two teams campaigning the No. 10 and No. 60, respectively.

Despite the shift in team structure, the ARX-05 would see multiple drivers return to continue racing with the car. Ricky Taylor, Alexander Rossi, and Helio Castroneves reunited as teammates at the 24 Hours of Daytona; Taylor raced the car full-time, Rossi raced at endurance rounds, and Castroneves ran Daytona only. Filipe Albuquerque was signed to be the second full-time driver. Dane Cameron, Juan Pablo Montoya and Helio Castroneves reunited in the No. 60 at the Petit Le Mans; Cameron raced the car full-time, Montoya raced at endurance rounds, and Castroneves raced at Road Atlanta only. Oliver Pla was the second full-time driver, racing every round except Road Atlanta, while A.J. Allmendinger was the third driver at Daytona.

The No. 10 began the season by achieving the ARX-05's most prestigious victory yet at the 2021 24 Hours of Daytona, winning by a margin of only 4.704 seconds over the No. 48 Action Express Racing Cadillac DPi-V.R. The No. 10 stayed a consistent threat to win races throughout the season, claiming six more podiums, including two wins at Mid-Ohio and Laguna Seca. The No. 60, meanwhile, proved less competitive, recording only two podium finishes.

The No. 10 narrowly lost out to the No. 31 Action Express Racing Cadillac for the 2021 team's championship, while the No. 60 finished fifth. Likewise, Acura finished 2nd to Cadillac in the manufacturer's championship.

#### 2022

The 2022 IMSA SportsCar Championship marked the final season for both the DPi regulations and the ARX-05. Wayne Taylor Racing retained Ricky Taylor and Filipe Albuqerque as full-time drivers, while Alexander Rossi returned for the 24 Hours of Daytona. Will Stevens joined the lineup at Daytona and Sebring, while Brendon Hartley competed at Road Atlanta. Meyer Shank Racing, introduced a new full-time lineup of Tom Blomqvist and Oliver Jarvis in the No. 60. Helio Castroneves returned for Daytona and Road America, Simon Pagenaud returned at Daytona, and Stoffel Vandoorne filled out the lineup for Sebring.

With the remainder of the DPi field consisting exclusively of Cadillac entries, the Acura teams remained competitive throughout the season. The No. 60 opened the season by giving the ARX-05 its second victory in the 24 Hours of Daytona, topping a double podium with the No. 10. The No. 10 amassed five wins throughout the season at Daytona qualifying, Laguna Seca, Mid-Ohio, Watkins Glen, and Road America, respectively, becoming the winningest team in DPi for the season. The No. 60, meanwhile, would not win again until the 2022 Petit Le Mans, but strung together five straight second-place finishes.

The No. 60 secured the DPi team's championship despite winning only two races, with its consistency proving decisive overall. The the No. 10 finished second in the standings despite claiming five victories. Both Acura entries finished ahead of every Cadillac in the teams' standings despite being outnumbered in the DPi field. As a result, Acura won the Manufacturer's championship once again, the win being the third for the ARX-05, tying them with Cadillac for most manufacturer's victories of IMSA's DPi era with one less season of competition.
