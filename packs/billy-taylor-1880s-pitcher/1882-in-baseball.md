## Notable seasons

- Buffalo Bisons first baseman Dan Brouthers leads the NL with 129 hits, a .368 batting average, a .950 OPS, and a 199 OPS+. His 63 runs batted in rank second in the league.
- Cincinnati Red Stockings pitcher Will White has a record of 40тАУ12 and leads the AA with 480 innings pitched, 40 wins, and 8 shutouts. He has a 1.54 earned run average and a 173 ERA+.

## Events

### JanuaryтАУMarch

- January 20 тАУ The state of Kentucky modifies a poorly written law that had banned baseball from being played in the state.
- February 25 тАУ In an effort to increase attendance, the Providence Grays will require their players and the opposition team to parade through the streets of Providence in full uniform on game days, accompanied by a brass band.
- March 11 тАУ After losing Dasher Troy and Sam Wise to the National League after signing contracts to play in the American Association, the AA drops its policy honoring the blacklist and expelled list of the National League.

### AprilтАУJune

- April 10 тАУ William Hulbert, the man most responsible for the founding of the National League and its sitting president, dies in Chicago. Arthur Soden, owner of the Boston Red Caps, is named Hulbert's temporary replacement for the 1882 season.
- May 1 тАУ The National League season begins.
- May 2
  - The American Association begins play with all 6 teams in action.
  - Charles Comiskey makes his debut with the St. Louis Brown Stockings.
  - John Clarkson makes his debut with the Worcester Worcesters.
- May 5 тАУ Cap Anson of the Chicago White Stockings is called out for walking back to the base after a foul ball was hit. The rule states the baserunner must run back to the base. The rule will be changed in the off season.
- May 6 тАУ Joe Hornung of the Boston Red Caps becomes the first player to hit 2 triples in one inning.
- May 25 тАУ Charles Foley of the Buffalo Bisons becomes the first major leaguer to hit for the cycle. Foley will also become the first player to hit 2 grand slams in one season in 1882.
- May 27 тАУ Mike Moynahan has his finger amputated at the first joint after breaking it during a game. Moynahan will hit .310 as the everyday shortstop for the Philadelphia Athletics in the American Association in 1883.
- June 1 тАУ The Worcester Worcesters lose at home 13тАУ3 in front of 50 people.
- June 5 тАУ In describing the Boston Red Caps 10тАУ2 win over the Detroit Wolverines, the Chicago Tribune states it is the first game in which a team scored 10 runs or more with all of them being earned.
- June 6 тАУ Blondie Purcell of the Buffalo Bisons is fined for cutting the game ball open, which was soggy, rendering it unusable. Purcell did it to force the umpire to put in a new ball so that Bison pitcher Pud Galvin could throw his curve more effectively.
- June 20 тАУ Larry Corcoran, pitcher for the Chicago White Stockings, goes 4тАУ4 at the plate, including the first grand slam in the history of the White Stockings. It is the only home run of Corcoran's career.
- June 22 тАУ The Cincinnati Red Stockings defeat the Pittsburgh Alleghenys on a game-ending triple play in the bottom of the 14th inning.

### JulyтАУSeptember

- July 6 тАУ In a game reminiscent of the old National Association, the St. Louis Brown Stockings defeat the Louisville Eclipse by a score of 21тАУ17.
- July 18 тАУ Tony Mullane of the Louisville Eclipse, begins switch-pitching in the 4th inning of a game against the Baltimore Orioles. Mullane pitches left-handed against left-handed batters and right-handed against right-handed batters. Mullane gives up a 2-out homer to Charlie Householder in the 9th and loses 9тАУ8.
- July 24 тАУ Seven Chicago White Stockings collect at least 4 hits and six score at least 4 runs in Chicago's 35тАУ4 pasting of the Cleveland Blues.
- August 17 тАУ The Providence Grays beat the Detroit Wolverines 1тАУ0 in 18 innings. Hall-of-Famer John Montgomery Ward goes the distance for the Grays, while fellow pitcher, teammate and Hall-of-Famer Charley Radbourn, playing right field on his non-pitching day, hits a home run in the 18th to win it. Some consider this the finest played game of the entire 19th century.
- August 18 тАУ Frank Mountain of the Worcester Worcesters and Jim McCormick of the Cleveland Blues become the first pitchers to hit a home run off each other in a game.
- September 2 тАУ The Cincinnati Red Stockings defeat the Louisville Eclipse 6тАУ2 to clinch the first pennant in American Association history.
- September 5 тАУ The Baltimore Orioles play the first 4 innings of their game against Pittsburgh in street clothes because their uniforms have been delayed at the train station.
- September 11 тАУ Tony Mullane of the Louisville Eclipse pitches a no-hitter against the Cincinnati Red Stockings.
- September 19 тАУ Guy Hecker of the Louisville Eclipse is the second Louisville pitcher to throw a no-hitter in 9 days as he hurls one against the Pittsburgh Alleghenys.
- September 20 тАУ Larry Corcoran of the Chicago White Stockings throws a no-hitter against the Worcester Worcesters. It is the 2nd no-hitter of Corcoran's career and the 3rd no-hitter in 10 days in major league baseball.
- September 22 тАУ The National League announces that the Troy Trojans and Worcester Worcesters will be removed from the league following the season.
- September 25 тАУ The lame-duck Worcester Worcesters hold the first double-header while only charging one admission price. This idea will be used by virtually every major league club for nearly 100 years.
- September 28 тАУ The Chicago White Stockings win their 3rd consecutive National League pennant with an 11тАУ5 victory over the Buffalo Bisons.
- September 28 тАУ The Worcester Worcesters lose to the Troy Trojans 4тАУ1 in front of 6 people. The 2 teams are by far the worst in the league and have already been given the official boot from the National League.
- September 29 тАУ Worcester loses again to Troy 10тАУ7 in front of 25 fans in Worcester's last major league game.

### OctoberтАУDecember

- October 4 тАУ The Cincinnati Red Stockings of the American Association defeat the Cleveland Blues of the National League 5тАУ2 in an exhibition game. It is the first victory by an AA team after losing the first 22 times to the NL.
- October 6 тАУ In the first post-season meeting of league champions, the Cincinnati Red Stockings of the American Association defeat the Chicago White Stockings 4тАУ0.
- October 7 тАУ The Chicago White Stockings even their series with the Cincinnati Red Stockings with a 2тАУ0 victory. Cincinnati will drop out of the series under threats of expulsion by the American Association.
- October 28 тАУ The Philadelphia Athletics of the American Association announce that the club turned a $22,000 profit during the 1882 season. It is more than any team in the National League made and gives credibility to the fledgling AA.
- November 18 тАУ A court rules in favor of player Charlie Bennett over the Pittsburgh Alleghenys of the American Association. Bennett had signed a $100 agreement to sign a contract to play for Pittsburgh in 1883. Instead, he ended up re-signing with the Detroit Wolverines of the National League. This case will be a factor in the player/owner battles of 1889тАУ1890 that results in the formation of the Players' League.
- November 22 тАУ John Day, owner of the New York Gothams, proposes a resolution that would prohibit teams from signing players who had broken the reserve clause. Both leagues will ultimately adopt this proposal and turn the reserve clause from a protection against roster-raiding by other clubs into an item used against hard-bargaining players. The Gothams will join the National League in 1883.
- December тАУ Abraham G. Mills is elected the new president of the National League.
- December 6 тАУ The National League formally admits the New York Gothams and the Philadelphia Quakers.
- December 14 тАУ The American Association hires a permanent staff of umpires who will be scheduled, paid and overseen by the league rather than individual teams. It will eventually become the standard practice in every professional league, regardless of sport.
