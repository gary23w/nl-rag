# 1792 Kentucky gubernatorial election

The **1792 Kentucky gubernatorial election** was held on May 17, 1791, in order to elect the first Governor of Kentucky upon Kentucky acquiring statehood on June 1, 1792. Democratic-Republican nominee Isaac Shelby was elected unanimously by an electoral college vote.

Until 1800, Kentucky elected its governors through electors who were chosen via popular vote from the same districts as the Kentucky House of Representatives.

## General election

On election day, May 17, 1791, Democratic-Republican nominee Isaac Shelby won the election after being unanimously chosen by an electoral college vote, thereby gaining Democratic-Republican control over the office of Governor. Shelby was sworn in as the 1st Governor of Kentucky on June 4, 1792.
